#### Bio
Zoe loves making a sound that is totally unique to each individual game, meshing the genre with the setting, creating themes that change as the story does, and composing tracks and sound effects that could only belong to the specific characters they were created for. She also enjoys the puzzle of making seamless transitions between different music tracks and the more technical aspect of implementing audio (in fact, she coded this website herself!).
In addition to creating audio for games, Zoe has actually created a handful of games herself! She is proficient in C#, HTML/CSS, and knows a little bit of several other languages, like javascript and python.
In her free time, Zoe loves to read, hike, knit, and, of course, play games!

#### Bio
Zoe's first stage experience was with her local theater group when she was 11 years old. She quickly switched from onstage to back, spending the next several years gaining experience in almost every department, including directing, music directing, stage managing, and running tech (QLab who?). 
She wrote her first musical soon after she began composing, a one-act adaptation of <i>The Witches</i> by Roald Dahl, performed in 2017 with the permission of the Dahl Foundation. Since then, she's written several musicals and standalone songs, and graduated from Berklee with a minor in writing for musical theatre. Most recently she's been working as the bookwriter/lyricist on <i>Monsters, Boosicals, And You</i> along with composer Perri Webb, as well as working on lyrics for Stephen Glantz' stage adaptation of Joseph Green's 1936 film <i>Yidl With His Fidl</i>.
Zoe also attends as many live productions as she can! From professional to high school theatre, you can find her in the audience, asking cast members for their autographs.
